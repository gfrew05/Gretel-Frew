Photo
'Tramslation' University of Melbourne

I am completing my B.A. of Psychology at the University of Maryland with breadth studies in architecture, public policy, and well-being.

My work sits at the intersection of human behavior and physical space. How rooms shape moods, how environments support character development, and how material choices elevate lifestyle. I believe these disciplines inform each other, and I aim to use them in every project I take on.

This spring I was part of the Archimarathon Studio in Melbourne, where I worked on my coursework alongside other architecture students and Kevin Hui. Seperated only by a moving glass partition, was Austin Maynard Architects who shared their space, resources, and expertise. Within the studio we had conversations with Tom Kundig, Winy Maas, and others who have greatly inspired my design practice.

I actively ride and compete my team of horses, assist Fantail Farm LLC. operations, and am quietly building a creative brand with my sisters. I am drawn to regenerative systems, developmentally supportive spaces, material conscientiousness, and the idea that meaningful design is always interdisciplinary.

June 2026

75 Hard — What I've Learned So Far

I will finish on June 27th, only 18 days to go! This intense challenge is nothing short of a large commitment. Participants have to follow strict rules and complete a daily checklist. Why would I choose to do this during an intense semester on exchange (3 time demanding courses, new country, new studio)? To give myself a routine that set the pace for the semester. What I found: Discipline is deciding the answer in advance and not allowing for another. People will question your choices either way, it doesn't mean a choice is wrong. Two workouts a day require serious accountability and planning to keep your body functioning and recovering well. I have loved the process and benefits immensely, even if it meat staying up until 3 AM some nights to finish the tasks. Highly recommend! xx
